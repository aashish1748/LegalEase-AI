Rent Increases

Original: "Landlord may increase rent with thirty (30) days written notice to Tenant for any reason deemed appropriate by Landlord in their sole discretion."
Plain English: Your landlord can raise your rent with only 30 days notice, for any reason they want.
Why this matters: This gives the landlord unlimited power to increase rent. Most tenant-friendly leases limit increases to once per year or tie them to market rates.

Repair Responsibilities

Original: "Tenant shall be responsible for all repairs, maintenance, and replacement of any item within the demised premises exceeding One Hundred Dollars ($100) in cost."
Plain English: You must pay for any repairs that cost more than $100, even if it's not your fault.
Why this matters: This is unusual and unfair. Typically, landlords are responsible for major repairs unless damage is caused by tenant negligence.

Entry Rights

Original: "Lessor reserves the right to enter the demised premises at any reasonable time with twenty-four (24) hours prior written notice for inspection, repair, or showing to prospective tenants."
Plain English: Your landlord can enter your home with 24 hours notice for inspections, repairs, or to show the property.
Why this matters: While 24-hour notice is standard, the broad reasons for entry may exceed what's legally required in your area.
